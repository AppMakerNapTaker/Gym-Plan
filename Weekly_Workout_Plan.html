Upper Body A — Push Focus

Chest emphasis with complementary pulling. One press, one fly, one row — balanced and efficient.

Warm-Up (5–8 min)
🌀
Side-Lying Windmill
Thoracic spine · Chest opener · Shoulder prep
1 set × 8/side
▼

How to do it

1
Lie on your side with knees stacked and bent at 90°, arms extended forward at shoulder height
2
Keeping knees stacked and still, sweep your top arm up and over, opening your chest to the ceiling
3
Let your gaze follow the hand — look toward the ceiling
4
Pause at end range, breathe into the stretch, slowly return. Move slow — this is not a stretch to rush

Lats · Teres major · Shoulder prep
2 sets × 12–15
Moderate wt
▼

How to do it

1
Set cable high on the functional trainer, attach a straight bar or rope. Stand back one step, slight hip hinge
2
Arms almost straight, pull the bar down in an arc toward your hips — like closing a cape
3
Think "lats to pockets" — sides of the back doing the work, not your arms
4
Slow controlled return. Perfect shoulder warm-up before pressing

Main Work
💪
Incline DB Bench Press (30°)
Upper chest · Front deltoid · Triceps
3 sets × 8–10
90 sec rest
▼
Why 30°? A shallow incline hits the upper chest without dumping load onto the shoulder joint. Your main pressing movement of the week — use a challenging weight you can control for all reps.

How to do it

1
Set bench to 30°. Sit with DBs on your thighs, kick them up as you lean back
2
DBs at upper chest, elbows at 60–75° from your torso (not flared to 90°)
3
Press up and slightly together — don't lock elbows at the top, keep tension on chest
4
Lower slowly (2–3 sec) — feel the stretch across the chest at the bottom

🦋
Cable Chest Fly
Chest · Stretch-focused · Pec isolation
2 sets × 12–15
90 sec rest
▼
Different stimulus to the press above. A fly stretches the pecs under load — something a press can't do. Set both cables at chest height on the functional trainer, stand in the middle, and fly. Light-moderate weight only.

How to do it

1
Set both cable pulleys to chest height. Stand in the center, one handle in each hand, slight step forward
2
Slight bend in elbows (locked, not moving). Open arms wide until you feel a deep chest stretch
3
Bring hands together in front of your chest — imagine hugging a barrel. Squeeze chest at the end
4
Control the return — the stretch phase is where the growth happens, don't rush it

Single Arm Cable Lat Pulldown (kneeling)
Lats · Teres major · Biceps
3 sets × 10/side
90 sec rest
▼
No lat pulldown machine? Set one cable to its highest point on the functional trainer, attach a single handle, and kneel directly underneath. Identical movement, same results — and single arm work fixes left/right imbalances.

How to do it

1
Cable high, single handle. Kneel directly under it, grab handle with one hand overhead
2
Slight lean away from the cable to increase the lat stretch at the top
3
Pull the handle down toward your hip — drive elbow down and back into your pocket
4
Slow return — feel the lat lengthen. Complete all reps on one side before switching

Single Arm DB Row
Mid-back · Rhomboids · Rear deltoid
2 sets × 10/side
90 sec rest
▼
No seated row machine or seat. The single arm DB row is actually superior — you get a longer range of motion and can feel each side working independently. Use the bench for support.

How to do it

1
Place one hand and same-side knee on the bench. Other foot flat on the floor. Back flat, parallel to floor
2
Hold DB in the free hand, let it hang straight down from your shoulder
3
Row DB up to hip/lower rib — drive elbow up and back. Don't rotate your torso to get more range
4
Lower slowly — full arm extension at the bottom to stretch the back before the next rep

Main Work
🦵
Barbell Back Squat
Quads · Glutes · Core
3 sets × 8–10
2–3 min rest
▼
Your rack is right there — use it. Start lighter than you think you need to. Perfect form at moderate weight is far more productive than grinding ugly heavy reps.

How to do it

1
Bar on upper traps (not neck). Feet shoulder-width, toes out 15–30°
2
Big breath in, brace core (like taking a punch), unrack and step back
3
Break hips and knees simultaneously — sit back and down. Push knees out. Hit parallel or below
4
Drive through whole foot to stand. Squeeze glutes at the top. Breathe out at the top
▶ Watch Demo
⬆️
Barbell Romanian Deadlift (RDL)
Hamstrings · Glutes · Lower back
3 sets × 10–12
2 min rest
▼
Barbell vs DBs: Use the barbell — it lets you load heavier over time and is easy to set up in the rack. The RDL is NOT a conventional deadlift. You don't lower the bar to the floor. It's a hamstring stretch movement.

How to do it

1
Take the bar out of the rack, hold at hip level. Feet hip-width, soft knee bend
2
Push hips BACK — not down. Bar slides down your thighs and shins
3
Stop when you feel a deep hamstring stretch (around mid-shin). Back flat throughout
4
Drive hips forward to stand — squeeze glutes at top. This is a hinge, not a squat

🦿
Bulgarian Split Squat (DB)
Quads · Glutes · Single-leg stability
2 sets × 8–10/side
90 sec rest
▼
Expect to be humbled. Most people find this harder than squatting twice the weight. It fixes left/right imbalances and develops the single-leg strength that carries over into everything.

How to do it

1
Hold a DB in each hand. Place rear foot on the bench — top of foot facing down
2
Front foot far enough forward that your knee doesn't cave inward as you descend
3
Lower back knee toward the floor — torso upright. Front knee tracks over toes
4
Drive through the front heel to stand. All reps one side before switching
▶ Watch Demo
🔔
Kettlebell Swings
Hamstrings · Glutes · Core · Power
3 sets × 15
60 sec rest
▼
Hinge, not squat. Power comes from snapping the hips forward — not from bending and straightening your knees. One of the best posterior chain and conditioning exercises you can do.

How to do it

1
Stand feet shoulder-width, KB a foot in front of you. Hike it back between your legs like a football
2
Explosively snap hips forward — stand up hard and fast. Let KB float to shoulder height
3
Let it swing back down, hinge to absorb, immediately repeat
4
Arms are just guides — all power comes from the hips. Think "hip snap"

Cable Face Pull
Rear delts · Rotator cuff · Upper traps
2 sets × 15–20
Moderate wt
▼
Shoulder health essential — never skip this. Face pulls counteract the internal rotation from all your pressing. They're injury prevention disguised as an exercise.

How to do it

1
Cable at head height, rope attachment. Stand back, hold rope with thumbs facing you
2
Pull rope toward your face, separating hands as it comes in — elbows HIGH and WIDE
3
End position: elbows higher than shoulders, hands at ear level. Pause, squeeze rear delts
4
Control the return. Not about weight — about activation

Lats · Biceps · Rear deltoid · Full back
3 sets × max reps
2–3 min rest
▼
The best back exercise in the gym. Aim for 6–10 clean reps per set. When you can consistently hit 10, add a DB between your feet or use a belt. If you can't reach 6, use a resistance band for assistance.

How to do it

1
Hang from the bar — full dead hang, arms straight, shoulders packed (not just dangling)
2
Pull until chin clears the bar — drive elbows down and back toward your hips
3
Lower slowly all the way back to a dead hang — don't cut the range short
4
No kipping — every rep strict and controlled. Quality over quantity

10 reps / side
▼
One of the best core exercises you can do. Trains the deep stabilisers that protect your lower back — critical for anyone doing heavy squats and deadlifts.

Slowly lower one arm overhead AND the opposite leg toward the floor simultaneously
4
Just above the floor, return and repeat the other side. Lower back stays flat the entire time

20–30 sec
▼
The hardest ab exercise in this program. If you can hold 30 sec with perfect form, your core is genuinely strong. Start at 10–15 sec if needed and build up.

How to do it

Lower back must stay pressed into the floor — if it arches, bend your knees or raise your legs higher
4
Hold and breathe. If you can hold easily for 30 sec, add ankle weights or lower your legs

8-Week Progression

Weeks 1–2Learn exercises. Leave 2 reps in the tank. Build the habits.
Weeks 3–4Push to the top of rep ranges each session.
Weeks 5–6Add weight where you've maxed the rep range.
Weeks 7–8Max effort — aim for PRs on key exercises.
Week 9Deload — cut sets by 40%, keep same weight. Recover and grow.
